Classes

Ballet

A classical dance form demanding grace and precision and employing formalized steps and gestures set in intricate, flowing patterns to create expression through movement

Ballet is the foundation of all dance styles, you don’t have to take this class but we do strongly encourage everyone to take ballet as the technique delivered within these classes improves a students technical ability and they progress much quicker regardless of preferred dance style.

"I love ballet, it’s helped me improve my technique so much and I love that our teacher uses regular songs and not just classical music, it makes it more fun"

Alice, Grade 4 student

Jazz

This style combines techniques of classical ballet and modern dance with the current forms of popular dance. Jazz also has its own movement vocabulary ranging from the isolation of certain body parts to the movement of the entire body with the accents of musical rhythms.

Our jazz classes are very upbeat and fun with lots of technique to improve your dancing, the class has a mixture of kicks, leaps, turns and a small routine to get the students use to picking up quickly but more importantly accurately.

“Jazz is the best! I always look forward to learning new combinations and improving my turns, I couldn’t even do a turn when I first started!”

Jamie, Grade 6 student

Tap

This type of dance is characterised by using the sounds of metal taps affixed to the heel and toe of shoes striking the floor as a form of percussion, coupled with both characteristic and interpretative body movements.

Tap class is a wonderful way to teach the students to hear beats and learn to move with the rhythm, again more up to date music is used to create the beats with the feet and a little routine to get creative.

“Tap has helped me improve my musicality so much, having to listen carefully to the music and understand when and how to move my feet has helped with all the other dance classes I do”

Ella, Intermediate student

Strength & Technique

This class helps develop as the name suggests a dancers strength and technique. This is the class that will help develop the flexibility and strength needed to progress to a more advanced level in dance

“Before starting stretch & tech I couldn’t even touch my toes nevermind anything else, now I can do the splits which means higher kicks, it’s helped with my back flexibility which means I can be more graceful in ballet and it’s helping me build up strength in my feet ready for pointework, it’s tough at times but it’s so worth it!”

Jack, Level 3 student

Acrobatics

The skill, practice, or sport of executing gymnastic feats (such as somersaults and handsprings) without the use of apparatus

Our acro classes enable students to learn acrobatic / gymnastic skills in a safe way. The teacher is fully qualified to teach all levels of tumbling using a super safe inflatable airtrack. Tricks that will be learned will be amongst other things - aerials, back handsprings, walk overs etc

“I love acro class! Our teacher makes me feel comfortable and explains everything before assisting me in trying new tricks. I also love that the airtrack is like a bouncy castle, it’s fun but so safe!”

Rosie, Level 2 student

Commercial

The type of dancing you see when artists perform live, in music videos, on TV, Film and of course commercials.

Our commercial classes are fun, upbeat and a great way for students to learn all the latest moves they see in music videos, films etc learning all different styles from hip hop and krunk to commercial jazz.

“I love love LOVE this class! Every week we do something different and it’s so exciting to find out what style we will be doing each week.”

Nina, Level 4 student

Troupes

Competition Troupe

Dance routines that are learned with the aim of performing them competitively

This class is open to anyone, most schools will only let students at a certain level compete or they have to do a set amount of classes,or have been a student for a set amount of years. We don’t do that, when we say it’s for everyone we mean it!

“Competition class helps me put everything I've learned in technqiue classes into practice. I love that competitions are for everyone not just supposed elite dancers, everyone gets a chance to compete”

Lila, Beginner level student

Performance Troupe

Dance routines that are learned with the aim of performing them at events

Performance troupe is very similar to our competition troupe, in that students will learn routines to perform on stage. The only difference is that these are not for competitions, they are to give students the chance to perform in front of an audience without the competitive pressure. It's also ideal for those thinking of doing competitions who want to try out performing first or who are not ready/able to travel around the country competing.

“I really enjoy having the chance to perform, I enjoy dancing at our local fetes but also when we go to places like Disneyland to perform!”

Jayden, Troupe 2 student
